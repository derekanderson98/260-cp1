Potato Soup

I got this recipe from my mother-in-law. I enjoy soup and was dabbling in making some of my own. I mentioned this on the phone and she texted this recipe and another of hers to me to try. I have yet to try the other one but this soup seemed too good not to try! My wife enjoys it a lot as well, and as it's her mother's recipe I imagine it reminds her of her childhood.

You will need:

  • 4 tbs butter
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrot
  • 1/2 cup chopped celery
  • 4 large potatoes, diced
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 4 tbs flour
  • 2 cups half & half
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 4-6 slices bacon, cooked crisp and crumbled
  • large cooking pot with lid
  • stirring spoon
  • mixing bowl
  1. Mix half & half with flour in mixing bowl and let set so the flour gets fully absorbed.
  2. Melt butter in pot and sautee onions, carrots, and celery until tender.
  3. Add chicken broth, salt, and diced potatoes, bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover with lid, and simmer for 10-12 minutes or until potatoes are tender.
  4. Stir flour/milk mixture slowly into simmering soup and add ground pepper. Bring to a very gentle boil, which will thicken the soup.
  5. Remove from heat. Stir in bacon and serve.
