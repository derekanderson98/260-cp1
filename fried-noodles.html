Fried Noodles

This recipe is a favorite of my wife's. I learned the recipe on my mission and thought nothing of it until I craved it randomly. My wife smelled it and instantly wanted to try it. One bite and it was an instant hit: now I make it almost every week! Unfortunately this recipe involves a lot of guesswork, but on the bright side it is very flexible! If you don't like spicy dishes, you can easily substitute a milder sauce. If you don't like the vegetables or meat you can easily substitute them as well. It works whether your veggies are fresh or frozen. Serves two.

You will need:

  • 2 packages of ramen noodles
  • 1/2 cup chopped bell peppers, various colors
  • 1 cup diced onions
  • 1 all-beef frank, diced
  • vegetable oil
  • shcezuan sauce
  • 2 large eggs
  • large frying pan
  • spatula
  • small bowl
  • small saucepan
  • strainer
  1. If you are using fresh vegetables only, add a small amount of vegetable oil to the frying pan. Sautee the beef frank, bell peppers, and onions on medium heat using the spatula to move the mixture around until the onions are yellow and you can see slight amounts of browning on the vegetables. Remove from heat and put mixture into small bowl.
  2. Prepare the ramen in the saucepan as stated on the instructions, do not add seasoning. Strain.
  3. Put a small amount of vegetable oil in the pan and add the noodles. Fry on medium heat for about a minute, using the spatula to try to break clumps.
  4. Add the vegetable mixture back into the pan, using the spatula to mix until evenly distributed.
  5. Add the shezuan sauce, mix until evenly distributed.
  6. Using the spatula, make a small opening in the noodles and crack egg into it. Mix with the rest of the dish. Repeat for other egg and fry until eggs are completely cooked.
  7. Remove from heat and serve.
